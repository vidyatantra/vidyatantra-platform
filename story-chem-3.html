Chef Mira was in a panic. A strong wind had blown through the outdoor kitchen. A bowl of puffed rice had overturned into a bowl of cooked chickpeas. Another gust had knocked over a jar of salt into a bucket of sand. "Kabir!" she shouted. "We need to separate the puffed rice from the chickpeas, and get the salt out of this sand!" Kabir looked at the mixtures. The puffed rice was light and big; the chickpeas were heavy and small.

💡 Concept Check

Kabir notices the puffed rice is much lighter than the chickpeas. What separation method can he use that takes advantage of the difference in weight, using the wind?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The method is winnowing. Winnowing is used to separate heavier and lighter components of a mixture by wind or by blowing air. The heavier chickpeas fall straight down, while the lighter puffed rice is carried further away.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Kabir stood on a stool and slowly poured the mixture of puffed rice and chickpeas from a height. A breeze carried the lighter puffed rice into a separate pile. "It works!" Mira said. "Now, what about this salt and sand mixture? The grains look almost the same size, and the wind won't help with this."

💡 Concept Check

Chef Mira needs to separate salt from sand. Since both are solids, what liquid can Kabir add to the mixture to dissolve one component?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Kabir can add water. Salt is soluble in water, meaning it dissolves. Sand is insoluble. By adding water, the salt dissolves, leaving the sand behind.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Kabir poured water into the salt and sand mixture. He stirred it well. The salt disappeared, but the sand settled at the bottom. "Now we have salt water and sand," Mira said. "We need to get the sand out first." Kabir took a piece of fine cloth and poured the mixture through it. The muddy sand was trapped in the cloth, and the clear salt water passed through into a pot below.

💡 Concept Check

What is the name of the method Kabir used to separate the solid sand from the liquid salt water by using a cloth?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The method is called filtration. Filtration is a separation technique used to remove insoluble solids from liquids using a filter medium (like cloth or filter paper).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Mira placed the pot of salt water on the stove. "Now," she said, "we need to get the salt back." She turned on the heat and let the water boil. Kabir watched as steam rose from the pot. Soon, all the water was gone, and a layer of white, clean salt crystals was left behind at the bottom. "Amazing!" Kabir said. "The salt didn't disappear with the water!"

💡 Concept Check

What is the name of the process where a liquid is heated to change into vapour, leaving behind the dissolved solid? What happens to the water?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The process is evaporation. The water changes into water vapour (gas) and rises into the air, leaving the solid salt behind.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

With just minutes to spare, the lunch was ready. Kabir sat down, exhausted but happy. "That was a close one," he said. "We used wind to separate by weight, water to dissolve, a cloth to filter, and heat to evaporate." Mira nodded. "In a kitchen, just like in a science lab, knowing how to separate substances isn't just useful—it's essential."

💡 Concept Check

Imagine you had a mixture of iron filings (metal) and sawdust (light wood). Name two different methods you could use to separate them based on what you learned.

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

1. Magnetic separation: Use a magnet to pull the iron out.
2. Winnowing or Flotation: Blow air or add water; sawdust will blow away or float, while heavy iron will sink.
