Meera sat at the dinner table, looking at her plate. There was a bowl of rice, a dish of lentil curry (dal), a piece of roti, a spoonful of curd, and a small bowl of fruit salad. "Mamma," she asked, "where does all this food come from? I know we buy it from the market, but where does the market get it?" Her mother smiled. "That is a wonderful question, Meera. Everything on your plate comes from either plants or animals. Let's find out which is which."

💡 Concept Check

Meera wants to know the two main sources of food. What are these two sources? Give one example of a food item that comes from each source.

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The two main sources of food are plants (e.g., rice, dal, fruits) and animals (e.g., milk, curd, eggs, meat).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Meera pointed to the rice. "Rice comes from a plant, right? It's a grain." Her mother nodded. "Yes, rice is the seed of a grass plant. And the dal comes from plants too—it is made from pulses, which are seeds." Meera looked at the curd. "But curd comes from milk, and milk comes from cows. That means curd comes from animals!" She was excited. "So everything on my plate comes from either plants or animals. But what about salt? Salt isn't from a plant or an animal!"

💡 Concept Check

Meera correctly identified that rice and dal come from plants, and curd comes from animals. What is the source of salt, and what are the three main categories of food sources?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Salt comes from non-living sources (minerals) like seawater. The three main categories of food sources are: Plants, Animals, and Non-living sources.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The next day, Meera went with her mother to the market. At the vegetable stall, she saw potatoes, carrots, spinach, and cauliflower. "These are all plants," she said. Then she pointed to a jar of honey. "Honey comes from bees, so that's an animal product!" Her mother bought a piece of ginger. "What part of the plant is this?" Meera asked. "Is it a root or a stem?" Her mother was impressed. "That's a very smart question. Different foods come from different parts of plants."

💡 Concept Check

Name the plant part for each of these: potato, spinach, cauliflower, and ginger.

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Potato = Modified Stem (Tuber), Spinach = Leaf, Cauliflower = Flower, Ginger = Modified Stem (Rhizome).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

As they passed a milk stall, Meera saw a man pouring milk. "That milk comes from cows," she said. Her mother added, "Milk is an animal product, but do you know where cows get their energy to produce milk?" Meera thought for a moment. "From grass and hay? So cows eat plants to make milk. That means even animal products come from plants in a way!" Her mother nodded proudly. "Exactly. All food energy ultimately comes from plants, because plants are the only living things that can make their own food using sunlight."

💡 Concept Check

What is the process by which plants make their own food, and why are plants called producers?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The process is photosynthesis. Plants are called producers (autotrophs) because they can produce their own food using sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Back home, Meera made a chart. On one side, she drew plants—wheat, rice, fruits. On the other side, she drew animals—cow, hen, bee. At the bottom, she wrote: "All food comes from plants or animals, and plants get their energy from the sun." She showed her chart to her class. Meera learned that understanding where food comes from helps us appreciate nature and make healthy choices.

💡 Concept Check

Explain why all food energy can be traced back to the sun.

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Plants use sunlight to make food through photosynthesis. When animals eat plants, they get that stored energy. Therefore, the sun is the ultimate source of energy for all living things in the food chain.
