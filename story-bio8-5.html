The monsoon arrived and frogs began their deafening chorus. But Zara noticed something wrong-fewer frogs than last year, their calls frantic[cite: 159]. "Their pond was filled for construction," her grandfather said. "If they don't find water to breed, this year's tadpoles will never come." [cite: 160]

💡 Concept Check

Why do frogs need water to breed?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Frogs use external fertilization-eggs and sperm meet in water[cite: 162]. Water keeps the eggs moist and allows the sperm to swim[cite: 163].

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Zara watched a female frog release hundreds of jelly-covered eggs[cite: 165]. A male clung to her back, releasing milky fluid over them. "That's sperm," her grandfather said[cite: 166]. "Millions swim toward the eggs, but only one enters each." Zara counted the eggs-hundreds from just one female[cite: 167].

💡 Concept Check

Why do frogs lay hundreds of eggs at once?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Most eggs and tadpoles die from predators, disease, or habitat loss[cite: 169]. Laying hundreds ensures at least a few survive to become adult frogs[cite: 170].

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Days later, the eggs hatched. Tadpoles swam everywhere-round bodies, long tails, no legs[cite: 172]. Zara watched one grow hind legs, then front legs. Its tail shrank. Its head broadened[cite: 173]. It was becoming a frog before her eyes. "Metamorphosis," her grandfather said[cite: 174].

💡 Concept Check

What is metamorphosis in frogs?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Metamorphosis is the dramatic transformation from a tadpole (water creature) to an adult frog (land creature)[cite: 176].

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Weeks passed. Zara counted the tadpoles each day-fewer and fewer[cite: 179]. "Some were eaten. Some starved. Some died when water got too warm," her grandfather explained[cite: 180]. "Not every egg becomes a frog. That's nature's way." Only fifteen survived from hundreds[cite: 181].

💡 Concept Check

Why do so many tadpoles die?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Predators eat them, food runs out, ponds dry up, or water becomes too hot or polluted[cite: 183].

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

On the last monsoon day, fifteen tiny frogs sat at the water's edge[cite: 186]. One by one, they hopped into the grass. "Fifteen is enough," her grandfather said[cite: 187]. "They'll eat insects, sing next monsoon, and lay their own eggs. As long as one pond remains, frogs will come back." [cite: 188]

💡 Concept Check

What is the difference between oviparous and viviparous animals?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Oviparous animals lay eggs (like frogs or birds); viviparous animals give birth to live young (like humans or cows)[cite: 190].

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
