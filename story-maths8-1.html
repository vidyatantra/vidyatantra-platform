Aman and his friends were trying to divide 3 pizzas equally among 4 people. His math teacher explained that the share each person gets, 3/4, is a special type of number.

💡 Concept Check

What is the definition of a rational number?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

A rational number is any number that can be expressed in the form p/q, where p and q are integers and q is not equal to zero.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

One of the friends asked if they had 0 pizzas to share among 4 people, would that still count as a math problem?

💡 Concept Check

Is zero (0) a rational number? Why?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Yes, zero is a rational number because it can be written as 0/1, 0/2, etc., where the denominator is not zero.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The teacher then challenged them with a game of balancing scales using positive and negative numbers.

💡 Concept Check

What is the additive inverse of the rational number a/b?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The additive inverse of a/b is -a/b, because when you add them together, the result is zero.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Next, the teacher asked them to find a "reciprocal" multiplier that would turn their fraction into exactly 1.

💡 Concept Check

What is the multiplicative inverse of the rational number a/b?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The multiplicative inverse (or reciprocal) of a/b is b/a, because (a/b) × (b/a) = 1.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Finally, Aman wanted to slice a pizza exactly halfway between his piece (1/4) and his friend's piece (1/2).

💡 Concept Check

How do you find a rational number exactly halfway between two rational numbers, a and b?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

You find their mean (average). The number exactly between a and b is (a + b) / 2.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
