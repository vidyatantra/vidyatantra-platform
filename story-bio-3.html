Riya was worried about her science project. "Nana, I have to make a chart about plants, but I don't know much about them," she said. Her grandfather smiled. "Let's go to my garden. The best way to learn about plants is to look at them closely." They walked into the garden, which was full of different plants. There was a tall mango tree, a small rose bush, a tomato plant with yellow flowers, and a creeping pumpkin vine. "Are all these plants the same?" Riya asked.

💡 Concept Check

Riya sees different kinds of plants in the garden. How are plants classified based on their size and stem type?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Plants are classified as herbs (small, green tender stems), shrubs (medium, woody stems branching near base), and trees (tall, thick woody trunks).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Nana stopped in front of a tall sunflower. "Every plant has roots, stems, leaves, flowers, and fruits." Riya looked at the sunflower. "I can see the stem, leaves, and the big yellow flower. But where are the roots?" Nana gently pulled a small weed from the ground. "See these thin, thread-like parts? These are the roots. They grow underground." Riya touched the roots. "What do they do? Just hold the plant in the ground?"

💡 Concept Check

What are the main functions of roots? What are the two types of root systems?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Roots anchor the plant and absorb water and minerals from the soil. The two types are taproots (one main root) and fibrous roots (many similar-sized roots).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Next, Nana pointed to the stem of the sunflower. "The stem is like a highway," he said. "It carries water and minerals from the roots up to the leaves and flowers. It also carries food made in the leaves down to other parts." Riya touched the stem. "It's strong and green." Nana explained, "In trees, stems become hard and woody—we call it a trunk. The stem also holds the plant upright so the leaves can get sunlight."

💡 Concept Check

What are the functions of the stem in a plant? How does the stem help in transport?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The stem supports the plant (holds it upright) and transports water/minerals upwards from the roots to the leaves, and transports food downwards from leaves to other parts.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Riya then looked closely at a leaf. She saw thin lines running across it. "What are these lines, Nana?" she asked. "Those are veins," he said. "They carry water and food within the leaf. Leaves are the food factories of the plant." Riya remembered learning about photosynthesis in school. "Leaves make food using sunlight, water, and air!" she said. "That's right," Nana said. "And do you see the tiny pores on the underside of the leaf? They are called stomata. They let air in and out."

💡 Concept Check

What is the structure of a leaf, and what are the functions of veins and stomata?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Leaves have a broad lamina. Veins transport water and food within the leaf. Stomata are tiny pores on the underside that allow gas exchange (air in and out).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Finally, they came to the tomato plant. Riya saw yellow flowers and small green tomatoes. "Flowers are the most important part for reproduction," Nana said. He pointed to its parts. "The colourful petals attract insects. Inside, there are male parts that produce pollen and a female part that makes seeds. When pollen reaches the female part, seeds form inside." Riya was amazed. "So fruits are just ripened parts of flowers that contain seeds!" She ran home excited to start her project.

💡 Concept Check

What are the main parts of a flower, and what is their role in reproduction? How are fruits formed?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Main parts are sepals, petals, stamens (male), and pistils (female). Flowers help in reproduction. After fertilization, the ovary swells and develops into a fruit containing seeds.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
