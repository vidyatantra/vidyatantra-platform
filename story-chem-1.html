Rani lived on a farm with her Aaji. The harvest festival was approaching, and Rani wanted a new, light, and airy kurta to wear while dancing. Aaji pointed to the sheep in the field and said, "We have plenty of wool," and then to a basket of fluffy, white cotton bolls, "and we have this. But wool makes me sweat in the sun, and these cotton bolls look like a tangled mess!" Rani frowned. She needed to turn the fluffy cotton bolls into a smooth fabric, but she didn't know the first step.

💡 Concept Check

Rani finds that wool is too warm for the festival weather. What is the first process she must do to separate the useful cotton fibres from the seeds inside the cotton bolls?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The first process is ginning. Ginning is the process of separating cotton fibres from their seeds. Without ginning, the fibres remain attached to the seeds and cannot be spun into yarn.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Aaji laughed and showed Rani an old wooden machine. "First, we must do this," she said, feeding the cotton bolls between rollers to pull out the seeds. Soon, they had a big pile of clean, fluffy cotton fibres. "Now it looks like the cotton I see in the shop," Rani said, "but it's just a loose pile. How do we turn this puff into a strong, thin thread that we can actually sew?"

💡 Concept Check

Rani has a pile of clean cotton fibres. What is the process of turning these loose fibres into a strong, thin thread called?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The process is called spinning. Spinning involves taking loose fibres and twisting them together to form a long, continuous, and strong thread called yarn.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Aaji brought out a takli (spindle). She showed Rani how to draw out the fibres and twist them together. Slowly, Rani learned the art of making a continuous thread. "Now we have yarn!" Rani exclaimed, holding up a spool of beautiful, hand-spun cotton thread. "But Aaji, my kurta is not made of thread; it is made of fabric. How do we turn this one long thread into a flat sheet of cloth that I can wear?"

💡 Concept Check

Rani has successfully made cotton yarn (thread). What is the name of the process (or the machine used) that interlaces two sets of yarns together to create fabric?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The process is called weaving, and it is often done on a loom. Weaving is the method of interlacing two sets of yarns (warp and weft) at right angles to form fabric.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Aaji set up her handloom. She carefully arranged many lengthwise threads and then used a shuttle to pass a crosswise thread over and under them. Click-clack, click-clack—slowly, a beautiful, flat piece of cotton fabric began to appear. Rani was overjoyed. But as she touched it, the fabric felt rough and dull. "It worked!" Rani said, "but this fabric looks nothing like the colourful clothes in the market. It's so plain!"

💡 Concept Check

Rani's woven fabric is strong and flat, but it is dull and grey. What processes are done to the fabric after weaving to make it smooth, attractive, and colourful?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The processes are washing, bleaching, dyeing (adding color), and finishing (like ironing) to make the fabric smooth, crisp, and ready for stitching.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Aaji took the fabric and washed it thoroughly to remove any dirt. Then, she dipped it into a pot of rich, red dye made from flowers. She hung it in the sun to dry. The final step was to iron it smooth. On the day of the festival, Rani wore the beautiful, red kurta. "My Aaji and I made it," she said proudly. "We started from the cotton bolls!" She had learned that making a simple piece of fabric involved many transforming steps.

💡 Concept Check

List the entire journey of the fabric in order, from the plant in the field to Rani's final dress.

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The journey is: Cotton bolls → Ginning (remove seeds) → Spinning (make yarn) → Weaving (make fabric) → Dyeing/Finishing → Stitching (make dress).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
