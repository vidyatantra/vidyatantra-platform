Kiran loved basketball. She practiced every day, but noticed some movements were easy and some were hard. Her elder sister Priya watched her practice. "You have good aim," Priya said, "but you need to work on jumping and turning." Kiran sighed. "How do I make my body move better? What actually makes us move?" Priya smiled. "That's a great question. Let me teach you about your skeleton—the framework of bones inside your body."

💡 Concept Check

What is a skeleton, and what are its main functions?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The skeleton is the framework of bones inside the body. It gives shape and support to the body, protects vital internal organs, and helps in movement.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Priya took out a chart of the human skeleton. "We have about 206 bones. The skull protects your brain. The backbone supports your body. Your ribs form a cage around your heart and lungs." Kiran touched her chest. "I can feel my ribs!" she said. "What about my arms and legs? How do they move so freely?" Priya pointed to her shoulder. "Your arm is attached to your body by a ball-and-socket joint. Let me show you the different kinds of joints."

💡 Concept Check

What are joints, and what are the main types of joints with examples?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Joints are where two bones meet. Main types: Ball and socket (shoulder, hip), Hinge (elbow, knee), Pivot (neck), and Fixed joints (skull).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Kiran tried moving her arm in circles. "That's my shoulder—ball and socket!" she said. Then she bent her elbow. "That's a hinge joint!" Priya nodded. "Now feel your backbone. It's not one single bone—it's made of 33 small bones called vertebrae. They are stacked with discs in between, which allows you to bend forward and backward." Kiran bent down. "So my spine is flexible? And between the bones, there is cartilage—a soft material that reduces friction."

💡 Concept Check

What is the structure of the backbone (spine)? What is cartilage and what is its function?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The backbone is made of 33 small bones called vertebrae. Cartilage is a soft, flexible tissue found between bones (and in ears/nose) that reduces friction and provides a cushion.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Now it was time to practice. "Bones alone don't move," Priya said. "You need muscles. Muscles are attached to bones by strong cords called tendons. When a muscle contracts (shortens), it pulls the bone and creates movement." Kiran flexed her arm. "When I bend my arm, my bicep muscle contracts. When I straighten it, my tricep contracts." Priya nodded. "Muscles work in pairs. One pulls the bone one way, and the other pulls it back."

💡 Concept Check

How do muscles work with bones to create movement? What are tendons, and why do muscles work in pairs?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Muscles are attached to bones by tendons. Muscles can only pull (contract), they cannot push. Therefore, they work in antagonistic pairs: while one contracts to pull the bone, the other relaxes.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

After understanding how her body worked, Kiran practiced with new focus. She knew that strong muscles would help her jump higher, and flexible joints would help her turn faster. She started doing stretches to keep her joints flexible and exercises to strengthen her muscles. At the next game, she played better than ever. "Now you're using your skeleton and muscles like a pro!" Priya cheered.

💡 Concept Check

Why is it important to keep our bones and muscles healthy? What can we do to maintain a healthy skeletal and muscular system?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Strong bones and muscles support the body and allow movement. To maintain them, we need a good diet (rich in calcium and protein), regular exercise, and stretching for joint flexibility.
