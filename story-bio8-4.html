Aanya found an old microscope in her grandfather's storeroom. She placed a drop of pond water on a slide and looked inside. [cite: 124] The world disappeared. In its place was a universe of tiny creatures darting, gliding, and spinning in a silent dance. [cite: 125] She gasped. [cite: 126]

💡 Concept Check

What is a cell and what did Aanya see? [cite: 127]

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

A cell is the smallest living unit of life. Aanya saw unicellular organisms-living creatures made of one cell. [cite: 128]

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Aanya sketched what she saw: a dark dot in the center, clear jelly around it, a thin outer boundary. [cite: 131] The dark dot was the nucleus. The jelly was cytoplasm. The boundary was the cell membrane. [cite: 132] Each part seemed to have a job. [cite: 133]

💡 Concept Check

What are the three main parts of a cell? [cite: 134]

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Cell membrane (outer boundary), cytoplasm (jelly-like substance), and nucleus (control center). [cite: 135]

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

At school, Aanya showed her teacher onion cells under the microscope. "They have a thick outer wall!" Aanya noticed. [cite: 138] "But the pond water cells didn't." Mrs. Rao smiled. "Onion cells are plant cells. Pond water cells are animal cells. Plant cells have something extra." [cite: 139]

💡 Concept Check

What is the difference between plant and animal cells? [cite: 140]

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Plant cells have a rigid cell wall; animal cells have only a flexible membrane. [cite: 141]

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Mrs. Rao drew a plant cell with tiny green dots. "Chloroplasts," she explained. [cite: 144] "They contain chlorophyll for making food. Only plant cells have them." Aanya looked at her onion drawing-no green dots. [cite: 145] "Onions grow underground. No sunlight, no chloroplasts!" [cite: 146]

💡 Concept Check

What are chloroplasts and why did onion cells lack them? [cite: 147]

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Chloroplasts contain chlorophyll for photosynthesis. Onion cells lack them because bulbs grow underground without sunlight. [cite: 148]

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

What is the difference between unicellular and multicellular organisms? [cite: 153]

Unicellular organisms have one cell doing everything; multicellular have many specialized cells working together. [cite: 154]
