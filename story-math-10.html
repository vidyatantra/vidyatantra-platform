Rohan and Meera decided to build a large kite shaped like a rhombus. They needed to buy wooden sticks for the outer frame. They measured each side to be exactly 50 cm long.

💡 Concept Check

What is the perimeter of the kite, and why is perimeter important for their project?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

Perimeter = 4 × 50 = 200 cm. Perimeter is the total distance around a figure. Knowing this tells Rohan exactly how much frame/border material to buy.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

To cover the kite completely without leaving holes, they needed to buy colourful paper. They knew they needed to calculate the area. They measured the inner supporting cross-sticks (the diagonals) as 80 cm and 60 cm.

💡 Concept Check

What is the area of this rhombus-shaped kite?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

Area = (80 × 60) ÷ 2 = 4800 ÷ 2 = 2400 square cm. The area of a rhombus is half the product of its diagonals.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

For the tail to flutter in the wind, they cut a long rectangular strip of paper. It was 100 cm long and 5 cm wide.

💡 Concept Check

What is the area of the rectangular tail?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

Area = 100 × 5 = 500 square cm. The area of a rectangle is calculated by multiplying its length by its width (or breadth).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

They also wanted to tape the edges of the rectangular tail so it wouldn't rip in the strong winds. They needed to find the total length of the border for the tail.

💡 Concept Check

What is the perimeter of the rectangular tail?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

Perimeter = 2 × (100 + 5) = 2 × 105 = 210 cm. The perimeter of a rectangle is twice the sum of its length and width.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Finally, Meera got curious. She wanted to compare the area of their rhombus kite to the area of a square kite that used the exact same amount of frame material (a perimeter of 200 cm).

💡 Concept Check

What would be the side length and area of that square?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

Side = 200 ÷ 4 = 50 cm. Area = 50 × 50 = 2500 square cm. (The square's area is slightly larger than the rhombus's area of 2400 cm²).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
