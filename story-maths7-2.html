A baker named Sofia was preparing for a festival. She needed 3/4 kg of flour for one cake and 2/3 kg of flour for another.

💡 Concept Check

What is the total flour required, and how does she add these fractions?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Total = 3/4 + 2/3 = 17/12 kg (or 1 5/12 kg). To add unlike fractions, find the LCM of denominators (12), convert to 9/12 + 8/12, and add numerators.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Sofia had 5/6 kg of sugar. She used 1/2 kg for cookies.

💡 Concept Check

How much sugar remains, and what operation is used?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Remaining = 5/6 - 1/2 = 2/6 = 1/3 kg. Subtract by finding a common denominator (6).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Each cupcake required 0.25 kg of batter. Sofia planned to make 24 cupcakes.

💡 Concept Check

How much batter is needed in total, and how is decimal multiplication performed?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Total batter = 0.25 × 24 = 6 kg. Multiply decimal by whole number: 0.25 is 25/100, so (25/100) × 24 = 6.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Sofia bought 2.5 kg of chocolate chips. Each batch of brownies used 0.4 kg.

💡 Concept Check

How many full batches can she make, and what operation is used?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Number of batches = 2.5 ÷ 0.4 = 6.25. So she can make 6 full batches. This uses division of decimals.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

A recipe required 1.75 liters of milk. Sofia had only 0.5-liter measuring cups.

💡 Concept Check

How many cups of milk does she need to measure, and how is this expressed as a division of decimals?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Number of cups = 1.75 ÷ 0.5 = 3.5 cups. She can measure 3 full cups and half a cup.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
