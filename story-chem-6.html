Maya and Rohan were helping clean up their school after a big festival. They looked at a huge pile of mixed garbage. Some of it smelled bad, like leftover food, vegetable peels, and dry leaves. The rest was made of plastic cups, glass bottles, and aluminium foil wrappers. "We can't just throw all of this into the same bin," Maya said. "The earth can break down the natural things, but the plastics will stay in the soil forever. We need to separate them into two groups."

💡 Concept Check

What are the two main types of waste Maya and Rohan need to separate? (Hint: Think about what can rot and what cannot).

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

They need to separate Biodegradable waste (things that rot/decay easily like food and leaves) from Non-biodegradable waste (things that do not rot, like plastics and glass).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

They put the fruit peels, dry leaves, and leftover food into a shallow pit in the school garden and covered it with soil. Rohan asked, "Why are we burying it? Won't it just stink up the garden?" Maya explained, "Under the soil, tiny microorganisms will start eating the waste. Over time, the garbage will completely rot and turn dark black. It will become a rich, natural fertilizer for our school plants."

💡 Concept Check

What is the process of rotting and converting plant and animal materials into dark, nutrient-rich manure called?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The process is called composting. Rotting of plant and animal waste converts it into a dark substance called compost (or manure), which is highly beneficial for plants.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

"We can even speed up the rotting," Maya added. "In my house, my mother keeps a special box for vegetable peels. But she doesn't just use soil. She added a bunch of special red-coloured earthworms to the box. They eat the organic waste and their droppings make the best compost in the world!" Rohan was amazed. "You have worms making fertilizer for you?"

💡 Concept Check

What is the specific method of preparing compost with the help of redworms called?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The method is called vermicomposting. Redworms eat the biodegradable waste and turn it into high-quality vermicompost very quickly.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Now they looked at the second pile: the non-biodegradable waste. It was full of empty plastic bottles, glass, and metal cans. "These can't go in the compost pit," Maya said. "They will stay exactly the same even after a hundred years. Instead, we must send them to the blue bins. Trucks will take them to factories where the plastic is melted down to make new bottles, and the paper is turned into new cardboard."

💡 Concept Check

What is the process of collecting waste materials and turning them into new, usable products at a factory called?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The process is recycling. Materials like paper, glass, metal, and certain plastics can be sent to factories to be broken down and manufactured into new items, saving resources.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Maya picked up a thick plastic bottle from the pile. Instead of tossing it in the recycling bin, she took out a pair of scissors and carefully cut the top half off. She placed it on the desk and put her pens and pencils inside. "You see," she told Rohan, "instead of throwing it away, or even using energy to recycle it, I found a way to use it exactly as it is." Rohan smiled. "That's brilliant! We managed to handle all the garbage."

💡 Concept Check

Maya used an old plastic bottle as a pen stand instead of discarding it. What is this method of waste management called?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The method is called reusing. Finding a new use for an old item (like turning a bottle into a pen stand or using old clothes for cleaning) is even better than recycling because it requires no factory energy.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
