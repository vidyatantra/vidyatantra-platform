Amir, an apprentice architect, was given a ruler (without measurements) and a compass. His first task was to duplicate a line segment exactly without measuring its numbers.

💡 Concept Check

What is the correct method to draw a line segment equal to a given line segment using only a compass and ruler?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

Place the compass points at the ends of the segment to copy the length. Then, on a new straight line drawn by the ruler, draw an arc with that compass setting to mark the exact endpoint.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

His next task was to construct a perpendicular bisector of a given line segment AB, cutting it exactly in half at a perfect 90-degree angle.

💡 Concept Check

What are the steps Amir should follow using his compass to build a perpendicular bisector?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

With compass radius more than half of AB, draw arcs from point A above and below the line. From B, draw arcs intersecting the first ones. Finally, join the intersection points.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The master then asked Amir to construct an angle of 60 degrees without using a protractor, relying purely on the circles of his compass.

💡 Concept Check

How can Amir construct a 60° angle using only a compass and ruler?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

Draw an arc from the start of a line. Keeping the same radius, place the compass on the intersection and draw another arc. Joining the start point to this new intersection builds an equilateral triangle (60°).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Later, Amir needed to construct a line strictly parallel to a given line, passing exactly through a specific dot floating above it.

💡 Concept Check

What construction method should Amir use to draw this parallel line?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

Draw a transversal line through the point. Then, use the compass to copy the corresponding angle at the new point. Equal corresponding angles guarantee parallel lines.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Finally, Amir was asked to construct a triangle with sides measuring exactly 5 cm, 6 cm, and 7 cm. He grabbed his compass, but the master stopped him and asked him to prove it was possible first.

💡 Concept Check

How can Amir ensure that these three sides form a valid triangle before constructing it?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

Check the triangle inequality theorem: The sum of any two sides must be greater than the third. (5+6 > 7, 5+7 > 6, 6+7 > 5). Since all are true, it is valid!

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
