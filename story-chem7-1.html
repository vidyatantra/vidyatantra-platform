A young chef named Arjun was experimenting in his grandmother's kitchen. He tasted a sour lemon and then a bitter soap solution. His grandmother decided to teach him about kitchen chemistry.

💡 Concept Check

What are acids and bases, and how do they differ in taste and effect on litmus paper?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Acids taste sour and turn blue litmus paper red. Bases taste bitter, feel soapy, and turn red litmus paper blue.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Arjun's grandmother gave him a strip of blue litmus paper. He tested lemon juice, vinegar, and tamarind. Each turned the litmus paper red.

💡 Concept Check

If all these substances turned blue litmus red, what does that tell us about their nature, and can we use a single indicator to distinguish between different acids?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

They are all acidic in nature. Litmus only tells us if it is an acid or base. We cannot use it to distinguish between different acids or measure their strength.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Next, grandmother showed Arjun a bottle of baking soda solution and a piece of soap. When tested with red litmus, both turned it blue.

💡 Concept Check

What is the common name for bases that are soluble in water, and what is an example from the kitchen?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Soluble bases are called alkalis. Baking soda (sodium bicarbonate) and soap are examples of alkalis found in the kitchen.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Arjun accidentally spilled some lemon juice. His grandmother sprinkled baking soda on it. The mixture began to fizz and bubble.

💡 Concept Check

What is happening when an acid and a base react? What are the products of this reaction?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

An acid-base reaction is called neutralization. The products are salt and water (and carbon dioxide gas in this case, causing the fizzing).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Grandmother then gave Arjun a cup of tea that had become too bitter. She asked him to add a pinch of tamarind to it. The bitterness reduced.

💡 Concept Check

How does adding an acidic substance like tamarind reduce the bitterness of tea, and what is this application called?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The bitterness is due to its basic nature. Adding tamarind (an acid) neutralizes the base, reducing bitterness. This is an application of neutralization.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
