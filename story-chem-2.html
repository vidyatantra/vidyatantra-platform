Anya found Veer sitting in the middle of a huge mess. He had a box filled with a metal spoon, a steel nail, a plastic toy, a wooden block, a glass marble, a rubber band, and a piece of chalk. He was trying to decide what to use for the sun. "It has to look shiny and bright, like it's glowing," Veer said, holding up the wooden block. "But this is dull." He held up the metal spoon. "This is shiny, but is it the right kind of shiny? I need to sort all this stuff first!"

💡 Concept Check

Anya needs to help Veer find a material with a "shiny" appearance. What is the property of materials that gives them a shiny appearance called? Which item has this property?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The property is called lustre. Metals typically have a shiny appearance (lustrous). The metal spoon and the steel nail have this property.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Anya picked up the glass marble and the steel nail. "Veer," she said, "I can see your face clearly in the marble, but when I hold the wooden block in front of my face, I can't see anything. This property might help us choose what to make the planets from." Veer wanted his planet Jupiter to look mysterious and blurry, while he wanted Earth to be clear and detailed.

💡 Concept Check

Anya is describing how light passes through materials. What are the three classifications of materials based on this property? Give an example for each.

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The three classifications are: Transparent (lets light through fully, e.g., glass), Translucent (lets some light through, e.g., thin plastic), and Opaque (blocks all light, e.g., wood/metal).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Veer decided to use the glass marble for Earth because it was transparent. He picked up the steel nail for the sun because it was lustrous. But then he tried to attach the glass marble to a cardboard sheet. He tried to bend the nail to make a hook, but it wouldn't budge. He tried to tie the rubber band around the marble, but the rubber band stretched easily. "Why is this so difficult?" Veer complained.

💡 Concept Check

What is the property that describes how easily a material can be hammered into a shape or bent? Which material in the story is likely the hardest and most difficult to change shape?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The property is hardness. Hard materials resist scratching and bending. The steel nail is the hardest material here and most difficult to bend by hand.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Anya helped him organize. "Okay," she said, "for the rings of Saturn, we need something that can bend easily but also hold its shape. The rubber band is perfect." Veer also needed to stick the planets to the board. He tried to dissolve the chalk in glue to make a paste, but it just sank. He noticed the plastic toy didn't mix with the glue at all. Only the wooden block seemed to soak up the glue a little.

💡 Concept Check

When Veer mixed chalk powder, it did not disappear evenly. What is the term for a material that completely mixes into a liquid? Are plastics and woods generally soluble or insoluble in water?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

A material that completely mixes in is soluble. Most solids like metals, plastics, and woods are insoluble in water (they do not dissolve).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Finally, Veer's model was ready. The sun was made from the shiny metal spoon, Earth from the glass marble, and Saturn's rings from the rubber band. He had sorted the materials based on their properties: lustre, transparency, hardness, and solubility. His teacher was impressed! "You didn't just build a model," she said, "you used scientific classification to choose the right material for the job."

💡 Concept Check

Why was it important for Veer to "sort materials into groups" before starting his project? What would have happened if he had chosen chalk to make Saturn's rings?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Sorting helps us understand material properties so we can choose the right one for a specific purpose. If he chose chalk for Saturn's rings, it would have snapped because chalk is brittle and cannot bend.
