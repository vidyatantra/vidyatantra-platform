In the bustling village of Malgudi, the headman Balu needed to conduct a census to arrange food and water for the annual fair. He sent young Aarav to count the residents across different colonies. When Aarav counted the weavers' colony, he found 1,248 people, and the farmers' colony had 2,356 people.

💡 Concept Check

To determine which colony had more people, how should Aarav compare these two numbers using place value?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

Aarav should compare the digits from the highest place value. The weavers' colony had 1 thousand, while the farmers' colony had 2 thousand. Since 2 thousand is greater than 1 thousand, the farmers' colony has more people.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Aarav visited the merchants' colony and estimated there were about 3,850 people, while the potters' colony had 3,742 people. Balu needed to round the numbers to get a quick idea of the supplies.

💡 Concept Check

If Aarav wanted to round the merchants' population (3,850) to the nearest hundred to make a quick estimate of the total, what number would he get, and why?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

3,850 rounded to the nearest hundred is 3,900. To round to the nearest hundred, we look at the tens digit. The tens digit is 5, which is equal to 5, so we round up.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Later, Aarav had to write the final population of the entire village in a report. The total came to twelve thousand four hundred fifty-six.

💡 Concept Check

How should Aarav write this number in numerals using the International system of place value commas?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

It is written as 12,456. In the International system, commas are placed after every three digits from the right.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

When Aarav submitted his report, Balu noticed that the population of the children's home was written as 567. He wanted to break it down for his ledger.

💡 Concept Check

If Aarav wanted to expand this number to show the value of each digit, how would he write 567 in expanded form?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

500 + 60 + 7. In 567, the digit 5 is in the hundreds place (500), 6 is in the tens place (60), and 7 is in the ones place (7).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Finally, Balu needed to order supplies for exactly 10,500 people, but Aarav's total was 10,489.

💡 Concept Check

By how much did Aarav's count differ from the required number, and was his count less or more?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

The difference is 11, and Aarav's count was less by 11 people. (10,500 − 10,489 = 11).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
