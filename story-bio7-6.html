Aanya noticed tiny new plants sprouting near a big mango tree. She asked how new plants were born, and her grandmother decided to teach her.

💡 Concept Check

What are the two main types of reproduction in plants?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The two main types are asexual reproduction (involving one parent) and sexual reproduction (involving two parents, through flowers and seeds).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Aanya's grandmother showed her a potato with "eyes" that were beginning to sprout, explaining new plants could grow from these.

💡 Concept Check

What type of reproduction is this, and what are these "eyes" actually called?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

This is a form of asexual reproduction called vegetative propagation. The "eyes" are buds located at the nodes of the stem.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Grandmother then took Aanya to a flowering plant and pointed to the delicate inner structures.

💡 Concept Check

What are the male and female reproductive parts of a flower, and what does each produce?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The male part is the stamen (produces pollen grains). The female part is the pistil, which contains the ovary (produces ovules).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Aanya noticed bees buzzing around the flowers. Grandmother explained that the bees were essential for the plants.

💡 Concept Check

What is pollination, and what role do insects like bees play?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Pollination is the transfer of pollen from the anther to the stigma. Insects carry pollen on their bodies from one flower to another.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

After some time, Aanya saw that the flower had withered and a small fruit was forming containing seeds.

💡 Concept Check

What happens after fertilization that leads to the formation of fruit and seeds?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

After fertilization, the ovule develops into a seed (containing the embryo), and the entire ovary swells and develops into a fruit.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
