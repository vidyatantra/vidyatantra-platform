In Tenali, a merchant sold perfume from a 1-liter flask. A customer wanted a small amount: 3 deciliters and 7 centiliters.

💡 Concept Check

How should the merchant write this quantity as a decimal of a liter, and what does each digit after the decimal represent?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

It is 0.37 liters. The digit 3 represents tenths of a liter (deciliters), and 7 represents hundredths of a liter (centiliters).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Another customer asked for a quantity equal to 1/4 liter.

💡 Concept Check

How can the merchant express 1/4 liter as a decimal, and what fraction with denominator 100 does it equal?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

It is 0.25 liters. As a fraction with denominator 100, it equals 25/100. (Multiply numerator and denominator of 1/4 by 25).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The merchant then had to add 0.37 liters and 0.25 liters for two customers who wanted to combine their orders.

💡 Concept Check

What is the sum, and how should the merchant align the decimals to add them correctly?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

The sum is 0.62 liters. The merchant should align the decimal points vertically before adding the digits in each column.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

A customer gave the merchant a gold coin worth 5.75 rupees, but the perfume cost 4.50 rupees.

💡 Concept Check

How much change should the merchant return, and how is decimal subtraction performed?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

1.25 rupees. Decimal subtraction is performed by aligning the decimal points and subtracting digit by digit from right to left.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Finally, the merchant needed to multiply the quantity 0.5 liters by 10 to fill a large order.

💡 Concept Check

What is the product, and what happens to the decimal point when multiplying a decimal by 10?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

The product is 5.0 liters (or 5). When multiplying a decimal by 10, the decimal point moves one place to the right.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
