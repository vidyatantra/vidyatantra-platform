Vikram and his classmates went on a field trip to a dense forest. His teacher explained that forests were complex, living systems supporting life on Earth.

💡 Concept Check

Why are forests called "biodiversity hotspots," and what does biodiversity mean?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Biodiversity means the variety of plants, animals, and microorganisms. Forests are "hotspots" because they support a vast number of different species.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Vikram noticed that the forest floor was covered with fallen leaves, rotting logs, and fungi. His teacher said this was essential.

💡 Concept Check

What role do decomposers like fungi and bacteria play in a forest ecosystem?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Decomposers break down dead plants and animals, returning essential nutrients to the soil so new plants can grow.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The teacher pointed to a stream. Vikram noticed that even though it had rained heavily, the stream was not flooded.

💡 Concept Check

How do forests help in maintaining the water cycle and preventing floods?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Canopies intercept rain, roots hold soil to stop erosion, and the forest floor acts like a sponge, absorbing water and releasing it slowly.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Vikram took a deep breath and realized how fresh the forest air felt compared to the city.

💡 Concept Check

How do forests maintain the balance of oxygen and carbon dioxide in the atmosphere?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

During photosynthesis, trees absorb large amounts of carbon dioxide (acting as carbon sinks) and release fresh oxygen.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The class met a forest guard who warned them about the tragic reality of cutting down trees.

💡 Concept Check

What are the consequences of deforestation, and why are forests called our lifeline?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Deforestation causes soil erosion, floods, loss of biodiversity, and climate change. They are our lifeline because they sustain all life on Earth.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
