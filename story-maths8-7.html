A sculptor was stacking small stone blocks to build a massive, perfectly proportional 3D monument shaped exactly like a giant die.

💡 Concept Check

What is a perfect cube mathematically?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

A perfect cube is a number obtained by multiplying an integer by itself three times (e.g., 2 × 2 × 2 = 8).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The side length of his monument was 12 blocks across. The sculptor needed to order the total number of blocks needed and wanted to predict the last digit.

💡 Concept Check

What is the unit digit of the cube of a number ending in 2?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

It will be 8 (because 2 × 2 × 2 = 8). Any number ending in 2 will have a cube ending in 8.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

He considered making the side length an odd number instead, like 15 blocks. His apprentice warned him that this would make the total block count odd.

💡 Concept Check

Is the cube of an odd number always an odd number?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Yes. The cube of an odd natural number is always odd, and the cube of an even number is always even.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The town mayor gave the sculptor a total budget for exactly 3375 blocks and asked him to figure out how wide the base would be.

💡 Concept Check

What mathematical operation is the inverse of finding a cube?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Finding the cube root is the inverse operation of finding a cube.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

To test his apprentice's understanding, the sculptor asked him an easier question: If we only had 1000 blocks, what would the side length be?

💡 Concept Check

What is the cube root of 1000?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The cube root of 1000 is 10 (since 10 × 10 × 10 = 1000).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
