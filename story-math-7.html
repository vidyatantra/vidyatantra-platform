Farmer Joseph had a rectangular piece of land. In his will, he gave half of it to his eldest daughter Eliza.

💡 Concept Check

What fraction of the land did Eliza receive, and how many equal parts was the land divided into to give her this share?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

Eliza received 1/2 of the land. The land was divided into 2 equal parts. Half means one out of two equal parts.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The remaining half of the land was then divided equally among his three other children: Tom, Sam, and Mia.

💡 Concept Check

What fraction of the original land did each of these three children receive?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

Each received 1/6 of the original land. Dividing the remaining 1/2 equally among 3 children means each gets 1/3 of 1/2. (1/3 × 1/2 = 1/6).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Tom wanted to compare his share with Eliza's to see if it was fair.

💡 Concept Check

Is 1/6 greater than or less than 1/2? How can Tom compare these two fractions?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

1/6 is less than 1/2. Tom can find a common denominator: 1/2 = 3/6. Comparing the numerators shows 1 is less than 3, so 1/6 < 3/6.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Sam decided to combine his share with Mia's share to plant a joint orchard.

💡 Concept Check

What fraction of the original land did Sam and Mia together own?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

They own 1/3 of the original land together. Sam had 1/6 and Mia had 1/6. 1/6 + 1/6 = 2/6. Simplifying 2/6 gives 1/3.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Later, a road was built that took up 1/12 of the total land from the portion that belonged to all the children together.

💡 Concept Check

What fraction of the land remained for the children after the road was built?

❌ Not quite! Math Explanation:

11/12 of the land remained. The children together owned all 1 whole land. After removing 1/12, the remainder is 1 − 1/12 = (12/12 − 1/12) = 11/12.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
