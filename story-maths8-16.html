A cryptographer was breaking a safe's passcode. The code was a 2-digit number written with letters 'ab'. She knew 'a' was the tens digit and 'b' was the units digit.

💡 Concept Check

How do you write a 2-digit number 'ab' in its generalized algebraic form?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

A 2-digit number 'ab' is written as $10a + b$ in generalized form (e.g., 52 = 10×5 + 2).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The first clue to crack the safe said: "My number can be split perfectly into pairs without any remainders."

💡 Concept Check

What is the divisibility rule for 2?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

A number is divisible by 2 if its ones (unit) digit is an even number (0, 2, 4, 6, or 8).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The second clue said: "If you add my individual digits together, that sum can be divided cleanly into 3 equal parts."

💡 Concept Check

What is the divisibility rule for 3?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

A number is divisible by 3 if the sum of its digits is a multiple of 3.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The cryptographer knew a similar trick for the number 9, which also involves adding all the digits together.

💡 Concept Check

What is the divisibility rule for 9?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

A number is divisible by 9 if the sum of its digits is a multiple of 9.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The final clue read: "I cannot be split into 5 equal groups without leaving leftovers." The cryptographer knew exactly what numbers to eliminate based on the last digit.

💡 Concept Check

What is the divisibility rule for 5?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

A number is divisible by 5 if its ones (unit) digit is either 0 or 5.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
