Kavya was sitting at her desk, staring at her science book. The question read: "What is air? Does it occupy space?" She looked around. "Air is just... nothing," she thought. Her mother walked in with a glass of water. "What's the matter?" she asked. Kavya pointed to the question. Her mother smiled. "Let me show you that air is not 'nothing.' It is a real substance that takes up space and has weight."

💡 Concept Check

Kavya thinks air is "nothing." What is air made of? Does air occupy space?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Air is a mixture of gases (78% Nitrogen, 21% Oxygen, 1% others like Carbon Dioxide). Yes, air occupies space and has weight, even though we cannot see it.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Her mother took a glass, a plastic sheet, and a tub of water. She placed the plastic sheet tightly over the glass and pushed the glass upside down into the water. The water did not enter the glass! "See?" she said. "The glass is filled with air. Air takes up space, so water cannot enter." Kavya was surprised. Her mother removed the plastic sheet slightly, and bubbles rose up as water slowly filled the glass.

💡 Concept Check

Describe the experiment that proves air occupies space. What happens when the plastic sheet is removed?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

When an empty glass is pushed upside down into water, the water does not enter because the air inside takes up the space. When tilted or the sheet is removed, the air escapes as bubbles, making room for water to enter.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Next, Kavya's mother brought out two balloons. She blew one up and tied it. She placed it on one side of a balance scale and placed the empty balloon on the other side. The inflated balloon was heavier! "Air has weight," her mother said. "That's why the inflated balloon is heavier than the empty one." Kavya was amazed. "So air is real—it takes up space and has weight!"

💡 Concept Check

The inflated balloon is heavier than the empty balloon. What does this prove? Does air have weight?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Yes, air has weight (mass). The experiment proves this because when air is added inside the balloon, it makes that side of the balance scale go down compared to the empty balloon.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Kavya's mother then lit a candle and placed it in a shallow dish of water. She covered the candle with a glass. Kavya watched as the candle flickered and went out. At the same time, water rose up inside the glass! "What happened?" Kavya asked. "The candle needed oxygen from the air to burn," her mother explained. "When the oxygen was used up, the candle went out."

💡 Concept Check

Kavya watches a candle go out when covered with a glass. Why does this happen? What does this show about air and burning?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The experiment shows that oxygen in the air is essential for burning. When the glass covers the candle, it uses up all the trapped oxygen and then goes out.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Kavya's mother then took her outside. "We breathe air all the time," she said. "When we inhale, we take in oxygen. When we exhale, we release carbon dioxide." Kavya took a deep breath. "So air is not just around us—it's inside us too!" Her mother smiled. "And not just us—all animals and plants need air. Plants use carbon dioxide to make food and release oxygen."

💡 Concept Check

Why do living organisms need air? What is the role of air in respiration and photosynthesis?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Living things need oxygen from the air for respiration (breathing to release energy). Plants use the carbon dioxide in the air to make food through a process called photosynthesis, releasing fresh oxygen back out.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
