A city park was shaped like a rectangle with length 80 m and breadth 50 m.

💡 Concept Check

What is the perimeter of the park, and how much fencing is needed?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Perimeter = 2 × (80 + 50) = 2 × 130 = 260 m. Perimeter of a rectangle is twice the sum of its length and breadth, which gives the fencing required.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Inside the park, there was a circular pond with a radius of 14 m.

💡 Concept Check

What is the area of the pond? (Use π = 22/7)

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Area = πr² = (22/7) × 14 × 14 = (22/7) × 196 = 22 × 28 = 616 m². Area of a circle is π times the square of the radius.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The park had a square-shaped children's play area with a side of 25 m.

💡 Concept Check

What is the area of the play area?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Area = side² = 25 × 25 = 625 m². Area of a square is side multiplied by side.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

A triangular flower bed in the park had a base of 12 m and a height of 8 m.

💡 Concept Check

What is its area?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Area = (1/2) × base × height = (1/2) × 12 × 8 = 48 m².

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The park had a circular walking track around the pond with a radius of 21 m.

💡 Concept Check

What is the circumference of the track? (Use π = 22/7)

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Circumference = 2πr = 2 × (22/7) × 21 = 2 × 22 × 3 = 132 m.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
