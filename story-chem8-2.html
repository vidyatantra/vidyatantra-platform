Riya was asked to identify materials in her house as metals or non-metals. She noticed that some materials were shiny while others were dull.

💡 Concept Check

What is a common property of metals?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Metals are shiny. This property is scientifically called lustre.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

She found that metals like iron could be hammered into very thin sheets.

💡 Concept Check

What is malleability?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Malleability is the ability to be hammered or beaten into thin sheets. It is a distinct property of metals.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

She also noticed that metals are widely used inside electrical cables.

💡 Concept Check

Why are metals used in wires?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Because they conduct electricity well. This allows electric current to flow easily through them.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

On the other hand, she found that materials like sulfur were brittle and shattered easily.

💡 Concept Check

What are non-metals?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Materials that do not have metallic properties. They are usually dull, brittle, and poor conductors of heat and electricity.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Riya understood that whether it's an iron hammer or a plastic handle, materials are chosen based on their specific properties.

💡 Concept Check

Why is it important to know material properties?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

To use them correctly. Different uses require different properties (e.g. you wouldn't use plastic for a frying pan bottom!).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
