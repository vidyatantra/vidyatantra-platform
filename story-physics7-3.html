Aryan and his friends loved to race their bicycles. One day, they decided to find out who was the fastest. They raced along a straight road, but there was a problem—they started at different times and finished at different times. How could they compare their speeds? Their science teacher, Mrs. Sharma, decided to turn this into a learning opportunity.

💡 Concept Check

What is speed, and how is it calculated? Why is it important to measure time accurately when comparing speeds?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Speed is distance traveled per unit of time (Speed = Distance ÷ Time). If you don't measure time accurately, even small errors will lead to incorrect speed calculations and unfair comparisons.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Mrs. Sharma gave each student a stopwatch and marked a fixed distance of 100 meters. Aryan recorded his time as 14.5 seconds. His friend Rohan took 16.2 seconds.

💡 Concept Check

What is Aryan's speed in meters per second, and who is faster?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Aryan's speed = 100 ÷ 14.5 = ~6.90 m/s. Rohan's speed = 100 ÷ 16.2 = ~6.17 m/s. Aryan is faster because he took less time to cover the same distance.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Aryan noticed that during the race, his speed was not constant. He started slow, then went faster, and slowed down near the finish.

💡 Concept Check

What is the difference between uniform motion and non-uniform motion?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Uniform motion is covering equal distances in equal time intervals (constant speed). Non-uniform motion covers unequal distances in equal time intervals (changing speed).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Mrs. Sharma explained how ancient people measured time before clocks existed. Aryan was fascinated by the different methods.

💡 Concept Check

What are some ancient devices used to measure time, and what were their limitations?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Ancient devices included sundials, sand clocks, and water clocks. Their limitations were lacking precision, and sundials wouldn't work at night or on cloudy days.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

The class discussed how speed is measured in vehicles. Aryan's father had a car with a speedometer and an odometer.

💡 Concept Check

What is the difference between a speedometer and an odometer, and what does each measure?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

A speedometer measures the instantaneous speed of the vehicle (in km/h). An odometer records the total cumulative distance the vehicle has traveled (in km).

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
