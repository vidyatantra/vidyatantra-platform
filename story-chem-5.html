Priya loved visiting her Nana's farm, but this year was different. The sun was scorching, and it hadn't rained in months. Nana had a small, stone water tank next to his vegetable patch. Every morning, he filled it with a bucket from the well. But by evening, Priya noticed the water level was much lower. "Nana, is someone stealing our water?" she asked. Nana smiled. "No, little one. The thief is invisible and lives in the sky."

💡 Concept Check

The water level in the tank is going down even though no one is using it. What is the process by which liquid water turns into water vapour and rises into the air?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The process is evaporation. The sun's heat causes liquid water to change into a gas (water vapour) and escape into the air. The water doesn't vanish—it just changes state.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Priya noticed that the water was only disappearing during the day, especially when the sun was directly overhead. At night, the water level remained almost the same. "It's the sun, isn't it, Nana?" she asked. "The sun's heat is taking the water away!" Nana nodded. "Yes. The water becomes something you can't see, floating in the air all around us. In the city, where it's hotter, this happens even faster."

💡 Concept Check

What is the name for the amount of water vapour present in the air? Why does evaporation happen faster on a hot, sunny day compared to a cool night?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The amount of water vapour in the air is called humidity. Evaporation happens faster on a hot day because the higher temperature provides more heat energy to the water molecules, allowing them to escape faster.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

That evening, as the air grew cooler, Priya was looking at Nana's glass of cold water. She noticed tiny drops of water forming on the outside of the glass. "Nana, look! Water is appearing on the glass, but it's not leaking!" Nana laughed. "This is the opposite of what happened to the tank. The cold glass cools the air around it. The invisible water vapour in the air touches the cold surface and turns back into liquid water."

💡 Concept Check

What is this process called, where invisible water vapour changes back into liquid water upon cooling?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The process is called condensation. When water vapour in the air hits a cool surface, it loses energy and changes back into liquid water droplets.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Priya's eyes widened. "Nana! I understand! The water evaporates and becomes vapour. The vapour floats up. When it gets high up, the air is cold. The vapour condenses into tiny droplets. Those droplets make clouds! And when the clouds get heavy, the water falls back as rain! The water is going in a circle!" Nana beamed. "That's exactly right. You just described the entire journey of water on our planet."

💡 Concept Check

Priya has just described the continuous movement of water on, above, and below the surface of the Earth. What is the name for this natural process?

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

The name is the water cycle. It is the continuous movement of water through evaporation, condensation, and precipitation, powered by the sun's energy.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Now that Priya understood the water cycle, she wanted to save Nana's tank water. The next day, she placed a floating wooden cover on top of the tank. At the end of the day, she checked the level. It had barely gone down! The cover slowed evaporation down by shading the water. "We can't stop the water cycle," Priya told Nana, "but we can be smart about how we save water until the rains come."

💡 Concept Check

Priya used a cover to slow down evaporation. Besides using a cover, name two other methods of water conservation that can help in areas facing a water shortage.

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Two great methods are Rainwater harvesting (collecting and storing rain from roofs) and Drip irrigation (delivering water directly to plant roots to stop wastage).
