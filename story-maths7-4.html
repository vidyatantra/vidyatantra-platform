A librarian named Riya found that a book had some pages torn. She knew that if she added 25 pages to the book, it would have exactly 150 pages. Let *p* represent the current number of pages.

💡 Concept Check

Write an equation for this situation and solve for *p*.

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Equation: p + 25 = 150. Solving: Subtract 25 from both sides, so p = 150 − 25 = 125 pages.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Riya then found another book where three times the number of pages, decreased by 10, equals 200. Let *x* be the number of pages.

💡 Concept Check

Write an equation and find the number of pages.

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Equation: 3x − 10 = 200. Solving: Add 10 to both sides (3x = 210), then divide by 3. x = 70 pages.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

A third book had 80 pages. Riya noticed that if she removed 5 pages from half of the book, she would get exactly 35 pages.

💡 Concept Check

Write an equation for this (let pages = *x*) and verify if it is true for 80 pages.

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Equation: (x/2) − 5 = 35. For x = 80: (80/2) − 5 = 40 − 5 = 35. So the statement is perfectly true.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Riya had to arrange books on shelves. She found that 4 shelves had an equal number of books, and the total books were exactly 96.

💡 Concept Check

Write an equation and find the number of books per shelf.

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Equation: 4y = 96 (where y is books per shelf). Solving: y = 96 ÷ 4 = 24 books per shelf.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!

Finally, Riya thought of a number, multiplied it by 5, added 12, and got exactly 47.

💡 Concept Check

What was the number? Form an equation and solve.

❌ Not quite! NCERT Explanation:

Equation: 5n + 12 = 47. Solving: Subtract 12 (5n = 35), then divide by 5. n = 7.

✅ Excellent! +100 Points!
